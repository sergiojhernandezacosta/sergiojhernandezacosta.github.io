I learned MATLAB through my school's curriculum. My first encounter with programming, in general, was my first semester of college, and it was MATLAB. Nonetheless, it was a good start in terms of learning logic and syntax.

MATLAB has been extremely useful in terms of executing calculations and creating simple applications. Initially, the school's MATLAB curriculum centered around constructing calculators related to particular engineering topics. For instance, we developed programs that could compute stresses, viscosity and drag in fluid dynamics, and operate within control systems. After seeing how much workload computer programming could reduce and optimize, I wanted to continue coding and create programs. On my own time, I was delving into data science and computational subjects extensively... I was using MATLAB for stock trading, machine learning, and engineering economic analysis, among other things.

In conclusion, I find MATLAB to be an extremely useful tool. What leaves me in awe is how versatile, faster, and mutually intelligible this programming language is.
