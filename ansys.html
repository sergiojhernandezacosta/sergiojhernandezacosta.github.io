My first uses of Ansys were not until later in my academic career. I first utilized Ansys (Workbench, specifically) in a course halfway through my college experience. It was for finite element analysis or FEA. The FEA Lab was considerably valuable for simulating and reducing time to calculate stress/deformation analyses.

After that, I eventually ventured into other analyses outside of static structural. The most important framework in Ansys Workbench has probably been computational fluid dynamics. I have gotten to use the CFD software for a wide range of computations.

My experience with Ansys extends to my classwork and my undergraduate research opportunity. For my research work, I specifically worked with Ansys Fluent. Moreover, the research work involved substantial simulation, both in terms of data and fluid mechanics. The research experience consisted of the modeling of the behavior of subterranean fluid (aquifers) and observing groundwater pollution. There were several constraints observed and experiments ran that were made possible by Ansys. Including pollution plumes, groundwater seepage pollution simulation, analyses of surface water and pipe flow, and laminar flow, just to name a few. Ultimately, getting to utilize the theory and knowledge from the classroom and apply it was satisfying.
