Learning HTML has been very intriguing and novel. Being a mechanical engineering major and wanting to venture into software, lean more towards coding (over hardware), I researched and sharpened programming skills pertinent to being a more skillful engineer. Therefore, I learned MATLAB, Python, C++, and other languages orthodox to most engineering and scientific endeavors. However, as I progressed through my academic career and read blogs from data scientists and engineers, I got the impression that web development would be a valuable addition to an engineer's arsenal.

I would say amidst several online courses and independent studying that I am familiar enough with front-end development. I can code and host static and responsive landing pages and a portfolio site (like this one!). All in all, I advocate for engineering and non-STEM majors alike to have some proficiency in web development so that you can—at most— be an even more versatile professional, or at the least create your own medium to display your work.
