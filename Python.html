I would characterize my experience with Python as extensive. This page will serve as a detailed post on the several projects I have undertaken using Python. First, I learned the language and polished on syntax after several Udemy courses and freeCodeCamp videos on data science and backend development. My school's curriculum taught mainly MATLAB and some C++; therefore, any languages outside of those two I proudly claim to have learned and studied on my own.

Much like the MATLAB page and my projects described there, my uses of Python have mainly involved data analysis and visualization, deep learning, and other computational/calculations related to engineering. Python is very convenient for pie charts, scatter plots, and bar graphs. Web scraping and other forms of data mining are fun and doable as well. Moreover, I like to make programs that can provide me results based on machine learning algorithms. For example, Python streamlines the calculations for linear regression, calculating the R2 value and interpolation/extrapolation. I have also delved into gradient boosting and other more complex machine learning techniques for my research program.

Python has been growing in popularity due to its robust and powerful range of uses. The available libraries are not only numerous but incredibly serviceable. I have experience with numerous libraries, namely: Tensorflow, pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib.
