I interned for the United States Department of Agriculture from May 2019 until the shutdown due to the pandemic, March 2020. The position I held was entitled Design Engineering Intern. I worked under research leader Dr. Robert Lascano and Dr. Tim Goebel. Essentially, the projects I assumed were to assist several aspects of my superior's research publications.





Some of my more notable responsibilities expanded my skills in 3D design/modeling, predictive data modeling, and machine learning algorithms. I designed several tools, parts, and apparatuses using Autodesk Inventor and subsequently uploaded the CAD files onto the 3D printer firmware. One of the projects involved a device that simulates rainfall. Aside from modeling the apparatus, I also analyzed the mass and volume flow rate of the simulated rainfall. Further, I even forecasted such properties utilizing the TensorFlow and NumPy libraries. Lastly, I got to familiarize myself with lab types of equipment for other projects: mass spectrometers, total organic carbon analyzer, and centrifuges. This experience was valuable in terms of getting immersed in the field of research. All in all, the internship was particularly insightful, and I believe served to establish fundamental skills that are imperative to the engineering workforce.
