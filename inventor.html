I would describe my experience with Autodesk Inventor as considerable. 3D modeling, rendering, and design are something I have been undertaking for quite some time now. My first encounter was in high school: in my sophomore year, I enrolled in an engineering graphics class. We first started with AutoCAD (two-dimensional isometric diagrams) and then progressed to Inventor.

The high school class gave me a good foundation for future collegiate courses... and not only that but for a career, as well. At this point, I have been using Autodesk Inventor for years now. My scope of experience includes drafting, assemblies (part mating, tolerances, constraints, scaling), and dynamic simulation.

As immediately as I started university, I was using computer-aided design software. From my first semester to my internship, CAD programs have been integral to my engineering/academic career.
